üìñ Reading ‚Äì 60 Minutes

This sub-test assesses your ability to read and understand texts commonly used in healthcare environments. It includes scanning for information, detailed reading, and interpreting meaning.

  • Part A: Four short texts on a single healthcare topic, such as patient information leaflets or instructions. Answer 20 questions in 15 minutes using skimming and scanning skills.
  • Part B: Six short workplace-related texts, like policies, memos, or emails. Each requires one multiple-choice answer (6 questions).
  • Part C: Two longer texts with detailed healthcare content, including studies or clinical guidelines. Answer 12 multiple-choice questions testing inference, opinion, and attitude.
Tip: Manage your time carefully. Spend no more than 1 minute per question in Parts B and C. Practice identifying main ideas quickly and eliminating incorrect options.

Additional advice: Highlight keywords or underline essential points in practice tests to improve focus and comprehension speed.

This sub-test involves two role-play scenarios between you and an interlocutor acting as a patient, carer, or relative. The focus is on effective communication in real healthcare contexts.

  • 3 minutes: Warm-up conversation (not assessed), where you discuss your professional background.
  • 2 x Role-Plays: Each role-play lasts 5 minutes, with 3 minutes preparation time. Scenarios reflect common healthcare situations relevant to your profession, such as explaining treatment plans or managing patient concerns.
Tip: Speak clearly and naturally. Use simple, patient-friendly language and demonstrate empathy. Avoid memorizing scripts; instead, be genuine and responsive as in real-life consultations.

Additional advice: Practice paraphrasing medical jargon into everyday language, ask open questions, and use appropriate body language if visible (smiling, nodding).

OET on Computer
  • Available both at authorized test centers and as a fully online proctored test you can take from home or anywhere with a reliable internet connection.
  • Requires basic to intermediate typing skills, especially important for the Writing sub-test. Familiarity with typing medical terminology is beneficial.
  • Results are delivered faster ‚Äî typically within 7 business days after your test date, allowing quicker next steps in your career or registration process.
  • The test content, format, question types, and scoring criteria are identical to the paper-based test, ensuring fairness.
  • Integrated tools like an on-screen timer, highlighting, and a digital whiteboard/note-taking area can enhance your test experience.
Tip: If taking the test from home, ensure your computer meets OET‚Äôs technical requirements, including a working webcam, microphone, and a quiet, private room free from distractions. Perform a technical check well before test day.
OET on Paper
  • The traditional test format is conducted in-person at official test centers, using pen and paper for all sections.
  • Ideal for candidates who are more comfortable with handwriting or who prefer making handwritten notes during the Listening and Reading sections.
  • Allows candidates to use physical paper to draft and edit the writing task, which some find helps organize thoughts better.
  • Results take slightly longer to process ‚Äî typically between 12 to 14 business days.
  • Note that paper test sessions may be limited or unavailable in certain regions, so check local availability early.
Tip: Make sure your handwriting is clear and legible to avoid any loss of marks. Practice writing quickly yet neatly to manage the writing time effectively without fatigue or smudging.
How to Choose Between Computer and Paper

Consider these factors to decide which test delivery mode suits you best:

  • Comfort with technology: If you type fast and are comfortable with computers, the online option may save time and speed up results.
  • Test location availability: If no nearby test center offers paper or online testing, check alternative options or travel requirements.
  • Test environment: Online proctoring requires a distraction-free environment at home; some candidates prefer the controlled environment of a test center.
  • Practice opportunities: Try sample tests in both formats to see which feels more natural.
Final tip: Regardless of format, thorough preparation and practice with the chosen mode will boost your confidence and performance.
